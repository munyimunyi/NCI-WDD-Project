Clean Water and Sanitation - International Cooperation

Target 6.5 (Internation Cooperation) aims to promote international cooperation in the field of shared water and sanitation. This goal is important for the world's population because it ensures that all people have access to clean water and sanitation services. One indicator for this is Goal 6.5.2. It measures how much of each country's shared water basins are covered by operational agreements for cooperation.

Goal 6.5.2 (Transboundary water cooperation) supports peace and regional integration because countries agree on how to share water, manage droughts and floods and exchange data on these. However, progress is still too slow to reach full coverage by 2030, as many shared basins in the world still do not have strong and operational arrangements in place.

Key Facts on Goal 6.5

  • Only 59% of global shared waters have active cooperation agreements, leaving a large amount without strong joint management.
  • 153 countries rely on shared water, showing it's a global issue affecting most of the world's population.
  • Cooperation arrangements help countries manage droughts and floods.
  • Progress towards this goal has been slow in recent years. Many regions still require strong agreements to reach full coverage by 2030.
